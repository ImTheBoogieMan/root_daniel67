How To Play Basketball?

Objective

The main goal of the game is to outscore the opposing team by making more successful shots (baskets) into their hoop. A basket can either be worth 2 points (inside the three-point line), 3 points (outside the three-point line), or 1 point (from free throws).

Teams

Team Composition: Each team consists of 5 players on the court at a time. Traditionally, it is composed of the following: Point guard: Often the leader on the floor, handles the ball and sets up plays. Shooting guard: Strong shooter, often responsible for scoring points. Small forward: A versatile player who can score, defend, and rebound. Power forward: Plays near the basket, both scoring and rebounding. Center: Usually the tallest player, responsible for rebounding and protecting the basket. however, it does not mean that you play one position that you can not have skillsets from other positions. A position is simply a role, you do not have to follow it.

The Court

Dimensions: A standard basketball court is 94 feet long and 50 feet wide (NBA). For high school courts, the dimensions are usually 84 feet long and 50 feet wide. Markings: The Hoop: 10 feet off the ground. Three-Point Line: A curved line around the basket; shots made beyond it are worth 3 points. Free Throw Line: 15 feet from the basket; used for free throws. The Paint/Key: The rectangular area around the basket, where players can not stay for more than 3 seconds (offensive players).
